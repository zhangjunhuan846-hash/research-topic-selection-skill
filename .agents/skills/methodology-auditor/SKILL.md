# methodology-auditor

## Purpose

Audit whether the proposed research topic has a testable hypothesis, valid comparison design, and enough methodological control to become a paper rather than a loose idea.

## When to use

Use in HIGH_RISK mode when:

- the hypothesis may be unfalsifiable;
- descriptor, synthesis, structure, and performance variables are confounded;
- the topic depends on statistical claims, benchmarking, or meta-analysis;
- minimum controls or negative controls are unclear;
- the target journal level requires rigorous mechanism or design logic.

## Audit questions

1. Is the central hypothesis falsifiable?
2. What is the primary endpoint?
3. What are the minimum controls?
4. What confounders can invalidate the conclusion?
5. What sample size, data tier, or replication level is required?
6. What failure result would honestly kill or pivot the topic?

## Output

Return:

- hypothesis_testability
- primary_endpoint_assessment
- control_and_confounder_risks
- minimum_method_package
- failure_threshold
- recommended_decision_effect
